Most social tools let anyone with the password publish straight to your accounts. Ours does not. Your team drafts the post, the person responsible reviews it, and only then does it reach the world. Built for businesses where a bad post is not just embarrassing, it is a problem.

In plain terms

The problem is not scheduling. It is sign-off

Plenty of tools will queue a post for Tuesday. Almost none of them handle the part that actually causes trouble: making sure somebody with authority read it first. So the password gets shared, the intern posts something, and by the time anyone notices, it is already public.

NexaFlow Digital Social puts the approval step in the middle, where it belongs. A creator writes. An approver signs off. Only then does anything publish. That rule is enforced by the software, not by asking people to remember.

How it works

How a post reaches the world

Four steps, and it cannot skip any of them.

01 — CONNECT

Link your accounts

Sign in to each service and pick the accounts this workspace publishes to. They stay yours, and you can disconnect any of them at any time.

02 — WRITE

Draft and submit

Anyone on your team can write a post, add an image, choose the channels, and set a time. When they are happy with it, they submit it for approval.

03 — APPROVE

Somebody signs off

An approver reads it and either releases it or sends it back with a note saying what needs to change. Until that happens, it goes nowhere.

04 — PUBLISH

It goes out

Approved posts publish at their scheduled time, or straight away if no time was set. Afterwards, the numbers come back so you can see what worked.

Questions

Common questions about social media management

Which accounts can I connect?

Right now: Facebook Pages, Instagram business and creator accounts, and Threads. You connect your own accounts, they stay yours, and you can disconnect any of them whenever you want. We add channels as we build them, and connecting a new one never changes how the rest of it works.

Do you post on our behalf, or do we?

You do. The software only ever publishes what somebody on your team has written and somebody on your team has approved. We do not write your posts or decide what goes out. If you would rather we did handle it for you, that is a separate conversation and we are happy to have it.

What stops a creator from just approving their own post?

The database refuses it. This is not a hidden button or a polite request. A person with the creator role physically cannot write an approved post, no matter what they click. Only an approver can release something.

What happens to our data?

Your posts and connected accounts are yours and are never shared with other customers. The access keys to your social accounts are stored on the server and cannot be read from a browser, including yours. You can delete everything at any time. The full detail is in our privacy policy and data deletion instructions.

What does it cost?

Fourteen days free, no card needed to start. After that, get in touch and we will scope it to the number of accounts and people you actually need rather than pushing a package.

Can you customise it for how we work?

Yes. That is the whole business. If your sign-off runs through two people, or you need it wired into a system you already use, that is exactly the kind of thing we build.
