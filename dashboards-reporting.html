In plain terms

Dashboards and reporting — what's the difference

A dashboard is the live view you check whenever you want — your key metrics, updating on their own, in one place. Automated reporting is the scheduled version: the report builds itself and lands in the right inbox every week, month, or cycle, without anyone assembling it by hand.

Most businesses need both, and the hard part is the same for each: getting scattered, half-trusted data into one source you can actually rely on. That's where we start — consolidating the data — so what you see is current and correct, not last week's guess.

Questions

Common questions about dashboards & reporting

What's the difference between a dashboard and a report?

A dashboard is a live view you check on demand — it's always current. A report is a snapshot built on a schedule and delivered to you, usually weekly or monthly. Many businesses use both: a dashboard for day-to-day visibility and automated reports for the regular updates leadership expects.

Where does the data come from?

From the systems you already use. We connect to your existing tools and data sources and consolidate them, so the dashboard reflects your real numbers rather than a separate set you maintain by hand.

Can the dashboard update in real time?

In most cases, yes — how live it is depends on your data sources, and we scope that with you up front so you know exactly how current the numbers will be.

Do you serve businesses outside Frisco?

Yes. We're based in Frisco, TX and work across DFW — Plano, McKinney, Allen, Carrollton, Lewisville, Dallas — and with clients nationwide.
